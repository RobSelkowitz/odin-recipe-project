A Ripping Lentil Soup!

This soup is great for an afternoon concert in Apu's Secret Garden. But don't serve it at the wedding if you're marrying a carrot; the guests might recognize friends among the ingredients. Serves 2-3.

You'll need:

  • 2T vegetable oil.
  • 1 medium onion, chopped.
  • 1 cup carrots, chopped.
  • 2 sticks celery, chopped.
  • 1 clove garlic, crushed.
  • 1/2 cup lentils.
  • 1 bay leaf.
  • 1 T freshly chopped parsley.
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper, to taste.
  • 2 1/4 cup vegetable stock or water.
  • additional parsley (for garnish, optional).
  • lemon wedges (for garnish, optional).

Directions:

  1. Add the oil to a medium pot and heat over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion, carrot, and celery and stir. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the onion is mostly translucent (about 5 minutes).
  3. Add the garlic and parsley and stir. Cook 1 more minute until fragrant. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Add the lentils and stir. Add the broth and bay leaf. Bring to a boil and cover.
  5. Reduce to a simmer. Cook lentils according to the package directions. Whole green lentils typically take 40-45 minutes.
  6. Taste for doneness and seasoning. Adjust salt and pepper as needed.
  7. Serve in soup bowls with additional parsley and a lemon wedge.

Pro Tips:

  • Add a pinch of cumin or paprika alongside the onion to add a smoky flavor.
  • Try different types of lentils until you find your favorite.
  • Top with grated Parmesean cheese or a dollop of sour cream to enhance the flavor. Use vegan substitutes if you want to avoid Apu's scorn.
